#### Summary
> The Telling is the Creation myth of both the [[Tir Na Beatha Leaflet\|Tir]] and the [[The Tribes\|Tuatha]]. It details how the various Gods and Goddesses sat and spoke the world into being in the aftermath of a great battle, embodying their hopes and dreams for a better world.
> Every year, on the first day of [[Cultural Matters/Sociology/Time-keeping\|Midsummer Moon]], [[Tuatha\|Tuatha]] from across the land gather in the city of [[Geography/City/Aite-Briethe\|Aite-Briethe]] to celebrate this event with the festival of [[Religion/Observances/Feile Draoi\|Feile Draoi]].

#### Text
> *In the dusk aftermath of the great battle with the ancient Fomorians, the gods, wearied from their divine strife, gathered upon the verdant hills. The air crackled with the remnants of celestial energies, and the horizon glowed with the fading embers of celestial confrontation.*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/The Dagda\|The Dagda]], chief among the gods, felt the weight of his colossal club upon his shoulder, its once-fearsome might now dulled by the struggle. [[Religion/Pantheon/Morrigan\|Morrigan]], fierce goddess of war and fate, gazed across the battlefield, her eyes reflecting the somber victory won. [[Religion/Pantheon/Aengus\|Aengus]], god of love, sought solace in the embrace of nature, finding peace among the fallen leaves and blooming flowers.*
> 
> *As the gods lay amidst the earth's breath, they contemplated the echoes of mortality that danced in the corners of their immortal minds. [[Religion/Pantheon/The Dagda\|The Dagda]], with a heart heavy from the blood spilled, called upon his divine kin to speak of a world reborn, a world crafted from their shared visions.*
> 
> *"[[Religion/Pantheon/Morrigan\|Morrigan]]," he began, "Goddess of War and Fate, what threads of destiny shall weave our world? Speak, and let your prophecies guide the path ahead."*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/Morrigan\|Morrigan]], her voice like the rustle of leaves in the wind, foretold tales of battles to come, destinies intertwined, and the inevitability of life and death. Her words wove a tapestry of strife, but within it, the threads of knowledge and wisdom gleamed.*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/Aengus\|Aengus]], tender god of love, raised his voice. "In this new world, let there be life that blooms with love and peace. Nature's dance shall be a melody of life, and all beings, great and small, shall find harmony in its rhythm."*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/Dana\|Dana]], chief among goddesses and embodiment of nature, added her voice. "Let the land breathe with order, where cycles of life, death, and rebirth nurture the balance we seek. May the sun kiss the earth with warmth, and the moon guide the ebb and flow of existence."*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/Blodeuwedd\|Blodeuwedd]], goddess of flowers and wisdom, spoke of peace. "In the petals of flowers, let there be serenity. Wisdom shall blossom, and the fragrance of understanding shall fill the air. May the winds carry the whispers of knowledge to all corners of creation."*
> 
> *[[Religion/Pantheon/Caillech\|Caillech]], goddess of winter and old age, embraced the Trickster's touch. "In the shadows of twilight, let there be mystery and cunning. Trickery shall be the spark that ignites change, and in the shadows, secrets shall thrive."*
> 
> *The gods, one by one, spoke their desires into the fabric of creation. [[Religion/Pantheon/Govannon\|Govannon]], god of smiths, envisioned [[The Tribes/Dwarves\|dwarves]], sturdy and industrious, shaped from the heart of the mountains. [[Religion/Pantheon/Arianrhod\|Arianrhod]], goddess of the moon, whispered of the dance between night and day, while [[Religion/Pantheon/Taranis\|Taranis]], god of thunder, roared his desire for storms and battles.*
> 
> *As the gods spoke, the campfire's flickering flames seemed to mirror the divine energy swirling around the assembly. [[Religion/Pantheon/Ogmios\|Ogmios]], god of eloquence, wove their collective tales into a melody of creation. The gods, their fatigue lifting, found renewed purpose in the world they conjured with their words.*
> 
> *And so, beneath the celestial canopy, the gods forged a new reality, shaped by their desires, balanced by their domains. The night air resonated with the echoes of their voices, weaving a tapestry of life, love, wisdom, and perpetual change.*
> 
> *Each year, we [[The Tribes\|Tuatha]] gather and celebrate [[Religion/Observances/Feile Draoi\|Feile Draoi]], the creation of our beloved [[Tir Na Beatha Leaflet\|Tir]]. We pass this tale down through generations, a testament to the gods' reflections and the birth of a world where mortal and divine threads intertwined.*
