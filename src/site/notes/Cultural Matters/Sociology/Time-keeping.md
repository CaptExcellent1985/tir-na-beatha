#### Summary
> The [[The Tuatha\|Tuatha]] of the [[Tir Na Beatha\|Tir]] have several terms they use to refer to the passage of time. They have several different era's when speaking of grand-scale histories, and the year is divided into 12 months. 
> Each month consists of 4 weeks, and each weeks consists of 7 days. 
> A year is thus equal to 336 days.

#### Detailed Summary
###### Eras
> There are two era's that the [[The Tuatha\|Tuatha]] may refer to:
> - Before [[Religion/Myths & Legends/The Telling\|The Telling]]
> - After [[Religion/Myths & Legends/The Telling\|The Telling]]
> 
>It is rare to hear common [[The Tuatha\|Tuatha]] refer to events after [[Religion/Myths & Legends/The Telling\|The Telling]] as that is considered pre-history and subject to much superstition. 

###### The Months
> | Name           | Tongue of the Ancients | Meaning |
> | -------------- | ---------------------- | ------- |
> | **Rime Moon**   | January                |     Rime refers to the grip of frost which holds the [[Tir Na Beatha\|Tir]] and keeps it fearful of [[Religion/Pantheon/Caillech\|Caillech]]'s trickery.    |
> | **Thawing Moon**   | February               |   As winter's grip loosens, this month is marked by the thawing of ice and of flowing water.      |
> | **Glimmer Moon**   | March                  |    Named for the subtle glimmer of magical energy that is believed to be more prevalent during this month.      |
> | **Elder Moon**     | April                  |     Dedicated to the Elder trees, considered sacred to the Fey, and the month in which they first begin to bloom.    |
> | **Meadow Moon**    | May                    |     This month is a celebration of life and all things vibrant as the meadows and forests of the [[Tir Na Beatha\|Tir]] spring fully to life.    |
> | **Swift Moon**     | June                   |    Named for the swiftness of Summer's arrival.      |
> | **Midsummer Moon** | July                   |    Marks the height of Summer and celebrated with many festivals amongst the [[The Tuatha\|Tuatha]]. Said to be a time of high activity for the Fey.     |
> | **Silver Moon**    | August                 |    A month dedicated to the mystical properties of silver. Fey enchantments are thought to be stronger, and silver is often used for protective charms     |
> | **Harvest Moon**   | September              |    As the harvest season begins, this month is a time of abundance and thanksgiving     |
> | **Frost Moon**     | October                |     As temperatures drop, the first frost arrives. This month is associated with the Fey's ability to weave frosty enchantments and illusions. |
> | **Shadow Moon**    | November               |    With the days growing shorter, this month is marked by longer nights and deeper shadows. Fey creatures are believed to move quietly and mysteriously.  |
> | **Dream Moon**     | December               |    Named for the long nights where dreams are said to be more vivid. This month is associated with divination and seeking guidance from the Fey through dreams.   |

###### The Days

| ------------------ | ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Lunar's Day        | Monday                 | A day dedicated to the moon and its mystical influence.                                                                                                         |
| Oak's Day          | Tuesday                | Named after the sacred oak tree, symbolizing strength and protection.                                                                                           |
| Thistledown's Day  | Wednesday              | Inspired by the delicate thistledown carried by the breeze.                                                                                                     |
| Stone Circle's Day | Thursday               | Reflecting the importance of stone circles to the Gods, this day is considered a time for gatherings, celebrations, and communing with the Fey.                 |
| Silver's Day       | Friday                 | Dedicated to silver, a metal believed to hold mystical properties.                                                                                              |
| Elm's Day          | Saturday               | Named after the elm tree, a symbol of intuition and interconnectedness. A day for seeking guidance from the Fey through dreams and visions.<br>                 |
| Sunfire's Day      | Sunday                 | Celebrating the power of the sun, believed to hold both life-giving and purifying qualities. Fey beings are said to bask in the sun's rays during this day.<br> |
